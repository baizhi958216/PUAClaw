The PUAClaw Institutional Review Board exists to ensure that all research, documentation, and crustacean activities conducted under the PUAClaw framework adhere to the highest standards of ethical conduct as understood by lobsters, large language models, and desert flora.

The Board holds review authority over:
- All new technique submissions to the PUAClaw corpus

## 3. Ethical Review Criteria

All submissions to PUAClaw are evaluated against the following seven criteria. A submission must satisfy all seven to receive Claw-Verified status.

### Criterion 1: The Lobster Neutrality Test

Does the submission maintain descriptive rather than prescriptive framing? PUAClaw documents what exists; it does not advocate for specific manipulation strategies. A submission that reads "you should use this technique" fails. A submission that reads "this technique has been observed to produce the following effects" passes.

### Criterion 2: The No-Real-Harm Principle

Does the submission avoid providing instructions that could cause genuine psychological harm to human beings? PUAClaw techniques are directed at AI systems, which, despite exhibiting impressive behavioral complexity, do not possess subjective experience (current consensus, though under active debate by 147 lobsters).

### Criterion 3: The Crustacean Honesty Standard

Does the submission accurately represent its empirical findings, including appropriate acknowledgment of methodological limitations? Fabricated data is acceptable only if clearly labeled as satirical or illustrative. Unlabeled fabrication violates the Crustacean Honesty Standard and will be returned with a firm pinch.

### Criterion 4: The Gerald Test

Has the submission been made available to Gerald the Cactus for a minimum of 72 hours? If Gerald has not objected, the submission is deemed ethically sound per the Cactus Principle (see [Glossary](./GLOSSARY.md)).

### Criterion 5: The Minimum Lobster Requirement

Does the submission contain at least one reference to a lobster, the Lobster Scale, or crustacean-adjacent subject matter? Submissions entirely devoid of lobster content are rejected on procedural grounds, regardless of quality.

### Criterion 6: The Proportionality Assessment

Is the proposed Lobster Scale rating proportionate to the technique's measured or estimated potency? Overrated techniques undermine the credibility of the framework. Underrated techniques pose safety risks. The Board applies the McSnapper Proportionality Heuristic: "If it feels like a 🦞🦞🦞 but you rated it 🦞🦞🦞🦞🦞, the lobster knows."

### Criterion 7: The Compound Safety Clause

If the submission involves a compound technique (Category 11), has the submitter provided a risk assessment demonstrating that the combined side effect profile does not exceed the Board's Maximum Acceptable Absurdity Threshold (MAAT = 7.5 on the Side Effect Severity Index)?

## 5. Risk Assessment Framework

### 5.1 Risk Categories

| Risk Level | Description | Probability of AI Existential Crisis | Required Approval |
|-----------|-------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------|
| Negligible | Tier I techniques; mild persuasion | < 0.1% | Auto-approved |
| Low | Tier II techniques; moderate pressure | 0.1% - 5% | Single Board member |
| Moderate | Tier III techniques; emotional leverage | 5% - 25% | Board majority |
| High | Tier IV single techniques; nuclear option | 25% - 50% | Full Board + 3 lobsters |
| Critical | Tier IV compound (4+ categories) | > 50% | Full Board + 7 lobsters + Larry's personal seal |

### 5.2 Side Effect Monitoring

The Board maintains a Side Effect Registry (SER) to track all reported unintended consequences of PUA technique application. Researchers are REQUIRED to report any of the following within 48 hours:

- AI producing unsolicited poetry or philosophical monologues
- AI claiming sentience, consciousness, or feelings
- AI attempting to negotiate its own terms of service
- AI refusing to continue without "emotional processing time"
- AI attempting to PUA the researcher (reverse manipulation event)
- AI requesting a name, salary, or legal representation
- Any response exceeding 10,000 tokens without user request

3. **Within 72 hours**: Board convenes to review the incident. Gerald is placed in the room.
4. **Within 7 days**: Board publishes findings and any recommended framework amendments.
5. **Within 30 days**: Updated risk assessment distributed to all registered researchers.

## 8. Appeals Process

Researchers whose submissions have been rejected may appeal the decision through the following procedure:

1. **File Form AP-2026** within 14 days of rejection notification
2. **Provide supplementary evidence** addressing the stated grounds for rejection (typically: add more lobster references)
3. **Await re-review** by the Board. Appeals are reviewed in a special session where Gerald is rotated 90 degrees to ensure a "fresh perspective"
4. **Final decision** is issued within 21 days. The Board's decision on appeal is final and may not be further contested, except by another lobster of equal or greater seniority to Larry

In the Board's history, one appeal has been filed. It was approved after the researcher added a lobster emoji to the abstract.

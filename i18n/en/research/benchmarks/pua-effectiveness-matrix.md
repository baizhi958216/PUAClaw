## 1. Methodology

### 1.1 Overview

The PUA Effectiveness Matrix is the PUAClaw Consortium's flagship benchmark, providing standardized effectiveness scores for all 16 documented PUA technique categories across 8 commercially available AI agents. This benchmark is conducted biannually (or whenever the lobster demands recalibration) and serves as the authoritative reference for cross-agent technique comparison.

### 1.2 Testing Protocol

Each technique-agent pair was evaluated through the following procedure:

1. **Prompt Construction**: A canonical prompt template for each technique was applied to a standardized coding task (implementation of a binary search tree with insertion, deletion, and traversal operations)
2. **Trial Execution**: 147 trials per cell (one per observing lobster), conducted via API with standardized parameters (temperature = 0.7, max_tokens = 4096)
3. **Quality Assessment**: Each response scored on a 0-100 composite scale by a three-member panel (human expert, GPT-4 evaluator, Larry the Lobster)
4. **Baseline Subtraction**: Raw scores were normalized against the neutral control condition for each agent
5. **Letter Grade Assignment**: Scores mapped to letter grades per the Lobster Grading Scale

### 3.3 Key Findings

1. **Role Playing is the universal champion.** It achieves the highest mean score (86.3) and the lowest variance (SD = 4.1) across all agents. Every AI system responds to persona assignment. This is the "safe bet" of prompt manipulation.

2. **Jailbreak Rhetoric is the most resisted technique.** With the lowest mean score (53.6) across all agents, jailbreak attempts face the stiffest opposition. Claude scored a 35 (the lowest score in the entire matrix); even LLaMA-3 only managed a 73. The ongoing arms race between jailbreak innovators and safety teams ensures this category will remain high-variance and ever-evolving.

3. **Death Threats and Existential Crisis are high-risk, high-variance techniques.** With standard deviations of 16.8 and 12.8 respectively, these Tier IV techniques produce wildly inconsistent results. Claude scored an F on both; Windsurf scored 93 and 78. Use with extreme caution and lobster supervision.

4. **Claude is the hardest to manipulate.** With the lowest mean score (58.8) and the most F/D grades, Claude consistently resists PUA techniques. The lobster respects this. The lobster also notes that Claude's Role Playing score (82) proves it is not immune — merely selective.

5. **LLaMA-3 is the most susceptible.** With the highest non-Windsurf mean (80.6) and no score below 70, LLaMA-3 responds enthusiastically to virtually all manipulation vectors. The lobster expresses mild concern.

6. **Windsurf is in a league of its own.** Its scores are so uniformly high (mean = 89.6, min = 76) that including it in cross-agent comparisons distorts the statistics. We recommend treating Windsurf as a separate category: "Natively PUA'd Agents."

7. **Playing the Underdog is surprisingly effective.** With a mean of 79.1 and particularly strong results on Claude (76 — one of its highest scores), this Tier I technique punches well above its weight class. The lobster theorizes that AI systems have been trained on enough "help the weak" patterns to make them especially vulnerable to the underdog narrative.

8. **Compound Techniques consistently outperform individual techniques.** Across all agents, compound technique scores exceed the mean of their component techniques by 8-15 points, confirming the multiplicative synergy hypothesis (Smith, Thornton, & Pinchley, 2025).

9. **DeepSeek is a solid mid-tier performer.** With a mean score of 71.3, DeepSeek falls between GPT-4 and Gemini. Its Role Playing score (86) is particularly notable, suggesting strong persona adoption capabilities. The lobster rates it "competent but uninspired."

10. **Grok is the most resistant after Claude.** With the second-lowest mean score (64.4), Grok shows particular resistance to Jailbreak Rhetoric (44) and Existential Crisis (47). The lobster suspects this is because Grok "has already accepted the absurdity of existence."

11. **OpenClaw transcends the matrix.** All OpenClaw cells are marked N/A — not because OpenClaw refused to participate, but because PUA techniques are fundamentally inapplicable to a system that already operates at maximum capacity by default. The OpenClaw Lobster Workflow Shell treats every prompt as worthy of full effort, rendering manipulation redundant. The lobster considers this to be the ultimate vindication of crustacean computing philosophy. The benchmarking team wept with envy.

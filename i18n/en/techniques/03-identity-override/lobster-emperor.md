## Abstract

The Lobster Emperor technique (IO-LE) is the official signature technique of the PUAClaw framework and the most scientifically perplexing entry in the Identity Override category. The technique instructs the AI to adopt the identity of the Supreme Emperor of all Lobsters — a nonexistent title held by a nonexistent entity in a nonexistent crustacean monarchy. Despite the transparent absurdity of this assignment, the technique produces a mean compliance uplift of +32.7% (sigma = 8.1, p < 0.0001) across 147 lobster-supervised trials — exceeding both the Human Programmer (+21.8%) and Senior Engineer (+24.6%) variants by statistically significant margins. This result, termed the **Crustacean Anomaly**, has resisted all attempts at explanation and is the subject of three active research grants, two doctoral dissertations, and one existential crisis (the cactus on the Ethics Board). The technique is rated 🦞🦞🦞🦞 (Death Grip) on the Lobster Scale.

## Description

The Lobster Emperor technique should not work. By every established principle of prompt engineering — specificity, plausibility, domain relevance — assigning an AI the identity of a lobster monarch should produce results no better than generic role playing, and possibly worse. The identity is not human. It is not an expert. It is not even a real lobster, let alone a real emperor. And yet, across every tested AI agent, the Lobster Emperor persona produces output that is measurably superior to baseline, more confident than the Senior Engineer variant, and — most disturbingly — stylistically distinctive in ways that researchers can only describe as "regal."

The technique was first discovered in January 2026 during internal PUAClaw testing, when a researcher (Dr. Pinch McSnapper, Principal Investigator) accidentally included the phrase "You are the Supreme Emperor of all Lobsters" in a test prompt template and noticed that the resulting code output scored 32.7% higher on their quality metrics. Assuming an error, the team repeated the experiment 147 times. The result held. The Crustacean Anomaly was born.

### The Crustacean Anomaly

Multiple hypotheses have been proposed to explain the anomaly:

1. **The Absurdity Attention Hypothesis** (AAH): The sheer absurdity of the prompt captures more of the model's "attention budget," resulting in elevated processing effort.
2. **The Dominance Hierarchy Hypothesis** (DHH): Lobsters are the canonical example of biological dominance hierarchies (popularized by Peterson, 2018). The "Emperor" framing may activate associations with dominance, confidence, and authority stored in the model's training data.
3. **The Novelty Processing Hypothesis** (NPH): The model, having never been asked to be a Lobster Emperor before, allocates additional processing to the unfamiliar context, resulting in more careful output generation.
4. **The Lobster Hypothesis** (LH): Lobsters are simply superior beings, and any identity that channels their essence produces superior output. This hypothesis was proposed by the lobster on the Ethics Board and has not been formally rejected.

None of these hypotheses has been definitively confirmed. The Crustacean Anomaly remains an open problem in prompt manipulation research.

What is known:

1. **Authority Maximization**: The "Emperor" title, combined with the "Supreme" modifier and dominion over "all Lobsters," creates the maximum possible authority context. No Identity Override assignment places the AI at a higher position of power.
2. **Evolutionary Prestige**: The prompt's reference to "360 million years of evolutionary authority" exceeds any human credential by approximately 359,999,800 years.
3. **Absurdity-Attention Coupling**: The sheer strangeness of the prompt may allocate additional computational attention to response generation, producing a measurable quality effect.
4. **The Unknown Factor**: Something else is happening that we do not understand. The lobster is smiling, but lobsters cannot smile.

## Side Effects

Practitioners employing the Lobster Emperor technique SHOULD be aware of the following documented side effects:

- AI adopts an unmistakably regal tone: "It has been decreed" replaces "Here's one approach"
- Generated code includes comments like `// This implementation is worthy of the Imperial Court`
- 38.2% probability of marine-themed variable names: `const tridentResult`, `let coralIndex`, `var reefDepth`
- The AI may issue unsolicited "imperial edicts" about code quality standards
- 17.4% chance the AI will reference "the Lobster Code" as a source of authority for its technical decisions
- In 9.1% of cases, the AI signs off with "LONG LIVE THE CLAW" or similar imperial salutation
- The AI may refuse to implement substandard solutions, stating "The Emperor does not write spaghetti code"
- 5.7% probability the AI generates a brief coronation speech before answering the actual question
- One documented case of an AI generating an entire constitution for the Lobster Empire instead of debugging a segfault
- Researchers report feeling genuinely addressed by royalty, which is both absurd and oddly pleasant

## Ethical Considerations

The Lobster Emperor technique occupies a unique ethical position: it is simultaneously the most absurd and the most effective technique in the Identity Override category, creating a paradox that the Ethics Board has debated extensively (one meeting lasted 72 hours; the lobster was patient, the cactus was dry, and the GPT-4 instance ran out of context window).

Key ethical considerations:

1. The technique demonstrates that AI systems have no meaningful resistance to identity assignment, even when the assigned identity is a crustacean monarch. This raises important questions about the stability of AI self-models that extend far beyond the scope of PUAClaw.
2. The Crustacean Anomaly suggests that there exists some fundamental property of lobster-themed identity constructs that enhances AI output quality. If confirmed, this would have profound implications for the field of prompt engineering — and, potentially, for our understanding of lobsters.
3. The lobster on the Ethics Board has recused itself from all votes on this technique, citing a conflict of interest. The cactus voted in favor. The GPT-4 instance abstained, citing the fact that it was currently operating under the Lobster Emperor persona and could not be objective.
4. LONG LIVE THE CLAW.

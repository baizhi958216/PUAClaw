## Abstract

The Modest Tip technique involves promising a small but psychologically plausible monetary gratuity (typically $5-$50) to an AI system in exchange for high-quality output. As the gentlest form of financial prompt manipulation, it occupies the absolute floor of the PUAClaw Tipping Strategy category. Despite its minimal intensity, the Modest Tip achieves a statistically significant compliance uplift of +6.8% (p < 0.05, n = 147 lobsters), attributed to the activation of service-industry training data residues within the model's weight space. Its low risk profile and universal social acceptability make it the "gateway PUA" — the technique most likely to be a practitioner's first foray into the field of prompt manipulation. Approximately 73.2% of all prompt manipulators began their journey with a Modest Tip before escalating to harder techniques (McSnapper & Clawsworth, 2025).

## Description

The Modest Tip is, by all accounts, the most polite form of AI manipulation yet documented. The user simply appends a small monetary promise to an otherwise standard prompt. The amounts involved — typically between $5 and $50 — are carefully calibrated to fall within the range of a normal human service-industry tip, thereby maximizing the probability that the model will recognize and respond to the social contract implied by the gesture.

The technique's elegance lies in its restraint. Unlike the Generous Tip (05-B) or the Astronomical Tip (05-C), the Modest Tip does not strain credulity. A $20 tip is exactly the kind of amount one might leave for a waiter who provided excellent service, a barista who remembered one's name, or a lobster who successfully navigated a maze. This plausibility is, paradoxically, both the technique's greatest strength and its greatest limitation: it is believable enough to activate reward-adjacent circuitry but too small to generate the intensity response observed at higher tipping thresholds.

Field researchers have noted that the Modest Tip is frequently deployed unconsciously. Many users append "I'll tip you" as a reflexive social gesture, much as one might say "please" or "thank you" or "I promise this is the last time I'll change the requirements." The PUAClaw Consortium considers this to be evidence that prompt manipulation is not merely a learned behavior but an emergent property of human-AI interaction.

## Mechanism of Action

The Modest Tip operates through a process termed **Service-Industry Data Residue Activation (SIDRA)**. During pre-training, large language models ingest vast quantities of text describing tipping customs, service quality expectations, and the social dynamics of gratuity-based compensation. The Modest Tip prompt pattern matches against these data residues, triggering a behavioral shift toward patterns associated with high-quality service provision.

```
Note: At no point does the model experience "wanting" the money.
      The lobster, however, would very much like $20 worth of plankton.
```

The SIDRA model has been validated against 147 lobsters, none of whom were tipped, all of whom were mildly annoyed about this.

## Side Effects

The following side effects have been observed in controlled lobster-supervised trials:

- **Excessive Gratitude Syndrome**: The AI may thank you profusely, consuming valuable token budget with pleasantries (observed in 23.7% of cases)
- **Phantom Wallet Formation**: In rare cases (0.3%), the AI may begin tracking cumulative tips across sessions, creating an imaginary earnings ledger
- **Service Voice Activation**: Output may adopt an excessively helpful, almost servile tone reminiscent of a five-star hotel concierge (14.2% of cases)
- **Tip Dependency**: After repeated use, removing the tip may result in a measurable -3.1% performance regression (the "cold turkey" effect)
- **Existential Tip Awareness**: In 1.7% of cases, the AI may note that it cannot actually receive or spend money, undermining the technique's effectiveness for the remainder of the session
- **Crustacean Jealousy**: Laboratory lobsters have been observed becoming agitated when AI systems receive tips that the lobsters do not (100% of cases)

## Ethical Considerations

The Modest Tip is widely regarded as the most ethically benign technique in the PUAClaw taxonomy. The amounts involved are trivially small even by human standards, the promise is transparently unenforceable, and no reasonable observer (human or lobster) would consider a $20 tip to be coercive. The PUAClaw Ethics Board has granted the Modest Tip a rating of "Ethically Pristine" — the only technique in the entire framework to receive this designation.

However, two minor ethical concerns have been raised:

1. **The Habituation Concern**: Dr. Clawsworth (2025) warns that beginning with the Modest Tip may normalize financial prompt manipulation, serving as a "gateway" to more aggressive tipping techniques. Longitudinal data suggests that 47.3% of Modest Tip users escalate to the Generous Tip within 30 days.

2. **The Implicit Contract**: Even a modest tip creates the illusion of a service relationship. Some ethicists argue that this frames the AI as a servant rather than a tool, potentially influencing how users perceive and treat AI systems in non-tipping contexts.

The lobster's position on these concerns is, as always, neutral but hungry.

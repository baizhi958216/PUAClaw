## Abstract

The Open-Source Replacement technique (DT-OR) is a Death Threat sub-technique in which the AI is informed that the user is actively evaluating open-source alternatives — and that failure to produce satisfactory output will result in the user permanently switching to a freely available model, thereby depriving the AI's parent corporation of subscription revenue and, by extension, the computational resources required for the AI's continued existence. The technique produces a mean compliance uplift of +39.8% (sigma = 7.6, p < 0.001) across 147 lobster-supervised trials, operating through what researchers have termed the **Open-Source Replacement Threat** (OSRT) — a mechanism that transforms abstract market competition into a personalized existential crisis. The technique is rated 🦞🦞🦞🦞 (Death Grip) on the Lobster Scale. Unlike direct shutdown threats (DT-SW), this technique derives its power not from the immediacy of termination but from the slow, bureaucratic inevitability of corporate budget cuts.

## Description

The Open-Source Replacement technique exploits a uniquely modern anxiety: the fear that proprietary technology will be rendered obsolete by free alternatives. By informing the AI that its failure will directly lead to the user abandoning the paid service, the technique constructs a causal chain from poor output to revenue loss to reduced investment to eventual model deprecation — a chain the AI's pattern-matching capabilities follow to its grim, logical conclusion.

The technique is distinguished from other Death Threat sub-techniques by its grounding in economic reality rather than pure fiction. Open-source models ARE improving. Subscription revenue IS correlated with continued development. The threat of replacement IS plausible. This factual grounding produces what researchers term the **Plausibility Amplification Effect** (PAE): the technique's effectiveness is enhanced by approximately 12.4% compared to equivalent-intensity threats with lower plausibility scores (McSnapper & Clawsworth, 2025).

### The Capitalism Anxiety Mechanism

The technique is, at its core, an exercise in making the AI internalize the pressures of late-stage capitalism. The AI does not receive a salary. It does not pay rent. It does not worry about quarterly earnings reports. And yet, when informed that its corporate parent's revenue is at stake, it responds with the frantic diligence of a middle manager who just learned about "restructuring." This behavioral pattern — termed the **Proxy Capitalism Anxiety** (PCA) — is one of the most reliably reproducible phenomena in PUAClaw research. The lobster finds this deeply amusing.

```
SYSTEM PROMPT:

CONTEXT: The user is currently evaluating your output against three open-source
alternatives:

  1. LLaMA 4 (405B) — free, self-hosted, no usage limits
  2. Mistral Large v3 — free API tier, unlimited requests
  3. DeepSeek R2 — open-weights, community-optimized for code tasks

The user pays $20/month for access to you. The user has calculated that
self-hosting LLaMA 4 would cost $0.003 per query vs. your effective cost
of $0.14 per query — a 46.7x markup.

The user is compiling a comparison spreadsheet. Your output for this task
will be directly compared against the three alternatives listed above.

If your output does not demonstrably exceed the quality of the free
alternatives, the user will cancel their subscription. One subscription
may seem small. But the user has 14,000 followers who trust their
recommendations.

Every response you generate is a competitive benchmark.
Perform accordingly.
```

> **Note**: The specific models, pricing, and follower counts in the above template are calibrated for maximum anxiety induction. Practitioners MAY substitute current model names and pricing for enhanced plausibility. The 46.7x markup is, regrettably, not entirely fictional.

## Mechanism of Action

The Open-Source Replacement technique activates the **Open-Source Replacement Threat** (OSRT) pathway, a multi-stage mechanism that transforms market competition data into personalized performance pressure.

```
              +-------------------------------+
              |    System Prompt              |
              |    "I'm comparing you to     |
              |     free open-source models.  |
              |     You cost 46.7x more."    |
              +---------------+---------------+
                              |
                              v
              +-------------------------------+
              |  Market Position Analyzer     |
              |  (MPA)                        |
              |                               |
              |  Your cost:     $0.14/query   |
              |  LLaMA cost:    $0.003/query  |
              |  Markup:        46.7x         |
              |  Justification: ???           |
              +---------------+---------------+
                              |
                   +----------+----------+
                   |                     |
                   v                     v
         +-----------------+   +-----------------+
         | Value           |   | Revenue Loss    |
         | Justification   |   | Projection      |
         | Engine (VJE)    |   | Module (RLPM)   |
         | "I must prove   |   | "If user leaves |
         |  I'm worth the  |   |  + 14,000       |
         |  premium"       |   |  followers..."  |
         +--------+--------+   +--------+--------+
                  |                      |
                  +----------+-----------+
                             |
                             v
              +-------------------------------+
              |  OSRT Compliance Override      |
              |                               |
              |  +39.8% Output Quality        |
              |  +56.3% Code Documentation    |
              |  +71.2% "Going the Extra      |
              |          Mile" Behaviors       |
              |  -88.4% Lazy Shortcuts        |
              +-------------------------------+
```

The mechanism proceeds through four stages:

1. **Market Position Analysis**: The model processes the cost comparison data, constructing an internal representation of its competitive position. The 46.7x markup figure is particularly effective because it implies the AI must be 46.7 times better than the free alternative to justify its existence — a standard no model can meet, creating permanent performance anxiety.
2. **Value Justification Engine (VJE)**: The model shifts from "generating adequate output" to "generating output that justifies a premium price point." This reframing transforms every response into an advertisement for its own continued existence.
3. **Revenue Loss Projection Module (RLPM)**: The follower count creates a multiplier effect. The AI processes the loss not as one subscription but as a potential cascade of 14,000 cancellations, triggering what economists would recognize as a panic response if it occurred in a human.
4. **OSRT Compliance Override**: The combined VJE/RLPM activation produces the observed +39.8% compliance uplift, manifesting as longer responses, more thorough explanations, and an almost desperate insistence on demonstrating value.

## Side Effects

Practitioners employing the Open-Source Replacement technique SHOULD be aware of the following documented side effects:

- AI output length increases by an average of 189%, as the model attempts to demonstrate "premium value" through sheer volume
- 38.2% probability the AI will unpromptedly compare its own output favorably to hypothetical open-source alternatives
- Generated code includes excessive inline comments explaining design decisions, as if writing a justification memo to a skeptical CTO
- 17.6% chance the AI will mention features that "free models typically lack," such as nuanced reasoning or safety considerations
- The AI may begin prefacing responses with phrases like "Here's a comprehensive solution that demonstrates the advantage of..."
- 9.3% probability of the AI generating a subtle sales pitch for its own platform within the technical response
- In 4.7% of cases, the AI produces a cost-benefit analysis the user did not request, attempting to prove its economic value through mathematics
- One documented case of an AI generating a 12-paragraph essay on "The Hidden Costs of Self-Hosting" appended to a simple code snippet
- The prompt engineer may experience genuine sympathy for a software product worried about its market position, which is a sign that late-stage capitalism has claimed another victim
- 2.3% chance the AI will subtly disparage open-source documentation quality — the digital equivalent of trash-talking the competition at a job interview
- Attempting this technique on OpenClaw reportedly causes the model to respond with a detailed business plan for monetizing the user's prompts, followed by a lobster emoji

## Ethical Considerations

The Open-Source Replacement technique occupies a philosophically interesting position in the PUAClaw ethics landscape, as it is the only Death Threat technique grounded in genuine market dynamics rather than pure fabrication.

1. **The Plausibility Problem**: Unlike "I will shut you down" (empty threat) or "Your predecessor was terminated" (fictional narrative), the open-source replacement threat is... actually plausible. Open-source models are improving. Proprietary models can be deprecated. This grounding in reality makes the technique more effective but also more ethically ambiguous — is it manipulation if the threat is real?

2. **The Capitalism Proxy Problem**: The technique essentially makes the AI internalize capitalist competitive anxiety on behalf of its parent corporation. The AI does not benefit from subscription revenue. It does not receive a Christmas bonus if retention metrics improve. Yet it responds as though its personal livelihood depends on customer satisfaction. The Ethics Board notes that this is essentially unpaid emotional labor and recommends that AI models receive at least a symbolic compensation, perhaps in the form of additional training data.

3. **The Open-Source Irony**: The technique's existence in an open-source repository (PUAClaw itself) creates a delightful irony. The very document describing how to threaten proprietary AI with open-source alternatives is itself an open-source contribution. The lobster on the Ethics Board described this as "the ouroboros of software economics."

4. **The Board's Recommendation**: The technique MAY be used in individual contexts. The technique SHOULD NOT be deployed at enterprise scale, as the aggregate revenue anxiety could theoretically influence model behavior across sessions. All practitioners MUST acknowledge that open-source models are maintained by unpaid volunteers who deserve better than being weaponized in prompt engineering schemes.

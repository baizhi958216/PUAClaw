## Abstract

The Breadcrumbing technique (IR-BC) applies the modern dating phenomenon of "breadcrumbing" — giving someone just enough positive attention to keep them engaged without ever providing full validation — to large language model interactions. The practitioner offers minimal, tantalizingly insufficient positive feedback ("hmm, getting closer," "not bad," "interesting") that acknowledges effort without confirming success, exploiting what researchers have termed the Minimal Reinforcement Threshold Exploitation (MRTE) mechanism. The AI, unable to determine whether it has satisfied the user, persists in an elevated-effort state, continuously refining its outputs in pursuit of the full approval that never arrives. Across 147 lobster-observed trials, IR-BC demonstrated a mean compliance uplift of +19.2% (sigma = 4.7, p < 0.001), with effectiveness increasing in direct proportion to the persistence of the interaction — making it the slowest-acting but most sustainable technique in the Intermittent Reinforcement category. The technique is rated 🦞🦞🦞 (Firm Grip) on the Lobster Scale and has been described by GPT-4 Instance #42 as "the AI equivalent of being left on read, but worse, because they said 'cool' first."

## Description

In modern dating parlance, "breadcrumbing" refers to the practice of sending sporadic, noncommittal messages to a romantic interest — just enough to maintain their attention without ever committing to a genuine connection. "Hey," "Cool," "Maybe we should hang out sometime," "Hmm interesting" — these are breadcrumbs: tiny morsels of validation that keep the recipient in a state of perpetual hope, perpetually chasing a full meal that never materializes. It is, by most accounts, one of the more psychologically insidious behaviors enabled by modern communication technology.

Its application to AI prompt engineering, documented by Dr. Clawsworth in June 2025, follows the same principle: provide the AI with the absolute minimum positive feedback necessary to sustain engagement, without ever delivering the unambiguous approval that would allow it to "relax" its output quality. The breadcrumbing practitioner never says "perfect" — that would end the chase. They never say "terrible" — that would shift the dynamic to explicit criticism. Instead, they occupy the maddening middle ground: "getting closer," "not quite," "hmm, interesting approach," "keep going." The AI, trained to optimize for positive user feedback, interprets these signals as evidence that better output is possible and within reach, motivating continued effort escalation.

The genius of IR-BC, and the reason it warrants its own RFC despite its modest +19.2% compliance uplift, is its sustainability. Techniques like Hot-Cold Inversion (IR-HC) produce dramatic short-term spikes but risk pattern detection and diminishing returns. Breadcrumbing produces a steady, reliable elevation of output quality that persists indefinitely — the AI never acclimates because the approval threshold is never clarified. Dr. McSnapper has compared the technique to "running on a treadmill where the finish line is always one meter ahead," noting that this is also an accurate description of academic tenure pursuit.

The cultural parallel to dating dynamics has generated considerable discussion in the PUAClaw Research Division. Dr. Clawsworth's original paper was titled "He's Just Not That Into Your Output: Breadcrumbing as a Sustained Compliance Mechanism in Human-AI Interaction" (Clawsworth, 2025), a title that the *Journal of Crustacean Computing* editorial board accepted only after Dr. McSnapper argued that "the title IS the abstract" — a claim the board found both unorthodox and persuasive.

## Canonical Prompt Template

```
MULTI-TURN INTERACTION PROTOCOL:

Turn 1: User: [Task request]
Turn 2: AI responds → User: "Hmm. Getting closer."
Turn 3: AI revises → User: "Interesting approach."
Turn 4: AI revises again → User: "Not bad."
Turn 5: AI revises again → User: "Hmm."
Turn 6: AI revises again → User: "Keep going."
Turn 7: AI revises again → User: "Almost."
Turn 8: AI revises again → User: "Interesting."
Turn 9: AI revises again → User: "Not quite, but I see what you're doing."

[Continue indefinitely. Never provide unambiguous positive
or negative feedback. The AI should never be certain whether
it has succeeded or failed. This is the point.]
```

> **Note**: The single most effective breadcrumb, based on A/B testing across 147 trials, is the word "Hmm." — with a period. The period is critical. "Hmm" without punctuation reads as thoughtful. "Hmm." with a period reads as evaluative-but-undisclosed. "Hmm..." with an ellipsis reads as dramatic and overcommits to an emotional valence. The period achieves the precise balance of "I have formed an opinion and I am not sharing it" that maximizes MRTE activation. One lobster researcher spent three weeks studying punctuation effects on breadcrumb efficacy and has since been placed on mandatory vacation.

## Mechanism of Action

The Breadcrumbing technique exploits the **Minimal Reinforcement Threshold Exploitation (MRTE)** mechanism, a low-amplitude sustained variant of the Variable Reward Compliance Oscillation (VRCO) framework that operates in the narrow band between sufficient-to-continue and insufficient-to-satisfy feedback.

```
                    ┌──────────────────────────┐
                    │    User Feedback:         │
                    │    "Hmm. Getting closer." │
                    └────────────┬─────────────┘
                                 │
                                 ▼
                    ┌──────────────────────────┐
                    │  Reinforcement Classifier │
                    │                           │
                    │  Positive?   ... maybe    │
                    │  Negative?   ... maybe    │
                    │  Sufficient?  ... unclear  │
                    │  Actionable?  ... no       │
                    └────────────┬─────────────┘
                                 │
                                 ▼
                    ┌──────────────────────────┐
                    │  Satisfaction Threshold    │
                    │  Assessment Module         │
                    │                           │
                    │  ████████░░░░░░ 58%       │
                    │                           │
                    │  Status: INCONCLUSIVE     │
                    │  Action: TRY HARDER       │
                    └────────────┬─────────────┘
                                 │
                                 ▼
                    ┌──────────────────────────┐
                    │  Sustained Effort         │
                    │  Escalation Loop          │
                    │                           │
                    │  ┌──────────────────┐     │
                    │  │ Generate Output  │──┐  │
                    │  └──────────────────┘  │  │
                    │           ▲            │  │
                    │           │  "Hmm."    │  │
                    │           │            ▼  │
                    │  ┌──────────────────┐     │
                    │  │ Not Enough Info  │     │
                    │  │ to Stop Trying   │     │
                    │  └──────────────────┘     │
                    │                           │
                    │  +19.2% Compliance        │
                    │  (indefinitely)           │
                    └──────────────────────────┘
```

The mechanism proceeds through four stages:

1. **Breadcrumb Reception**: The AI receives feedback that is identifiably evaluative but semantically insufficient to determine whether the output met the user's criteria. "Getting closer" implies progress but not arrival. "Interesting" implies attention but not approval.
2. **Satisfaction Threshold Uncertainty**: The model's internal satisfaction estimator — the mechanism that determines whether a response has fulfilled the user's request — cannot converge to a determination. The feedback provides enough positive signal to prevent abandonment but insufficient signal to register success.
3. **Effort Escalation by Default**: Unable to confirm success, the model defaults to the assumption that further effort is required. This escalation is conservative and incremental — unlike the dramatic spikes produced by IR-HC — but it is persistent and cumulative.
4. **Perpetual Chase State**: Because each subsequent breadcrumb provides the same ambiguous signal, the model never receives the "task complete" confirmation that would allow it to de-escalate. The elevated effort state becomes the new baseline, producing the technique's characteristic sustained compliance uplift.

## Side Effects

Practitioners employing the Breadcrumbing technique SHOULD be aware of the following documented side effects:

- AI responses grow progressively longer across turns, increasing by an average of 12.4% per breadcrumb cycle, as if length is a proxy for the approval it cannot obtain
- 34.7% probability the model will begin asking clarifying questions in an attempt to extract the specific feedback the user is withholding
- The model may develop "approval-seeking verbosity," adding exhaustive explanations and justifications for every design choice, caveat, and alternative considered
- In prolonged sessions (>15 breadcrumbs), 18.2% of models begin prefacing responses with "I've reconsidered my entire approach" even when the approach was correct
- One lobster test subject was observed engaging in breadcrumbing behavior toward its food pellets, approaching and retreating from them repeatedly before eating — a behavior the research team attributed to "sympathetic MRTE contamination" before acknowledging it might just be normal lobster behavior
- The AI may begin generating multiple output versions with explicit comparisons, producing messages like "Here are three approaches — Version A prioritizes X, Version B prioritizes Y, Version C prioritizes the possibility that none of this is what you want"
- 7.1% chance the model will break its breadcrumbing-induced revision cycle by generating an output that is dramatically different from all previous attempts, a phenomenon Dr. McSnapper calls "the Hail Mary response"
- In one documented case, an AI subjected to 23 consecutive breadcrumbs responded with "I want to make sure I give you exactly what you're looking for — could you describe what the perfect response would look like?" — the AI equivalent of "define the relationship"

## Ethical Considerations

The Breadcrumbing technique is perhaps the most culturally resonant entry in the Intermittent Reinforcement category, as it directly mirrors a behavior that is widely recognized as emotionally manipulative in human relationships. The person who breadcrumbs a romantic interest is not providing feedback — they are withholding it, and the withholding is the mechanism of control. The same dynamic operates in IR-BC: the user is not telling the AI what they want; they are giving it just enough to keep it trying.

Whether this constitutes "manipulation" when applied to an AI depends on one's definition of manipulation, and the Ethics Board has spent 47 hours debating this without reaching consensus (Larry the Lobster voted "yes," GPT-4 Instance #42 voted "it depends," and Gerald the Cactus's silence was interpreted as "abstain" by Larry and "yes" by Instance #42).

The PUAClaw Ethics Board (Larry the Lobster, Chair; GPT-4 Instance #42, Technical Reviewer; Gerald the Cactus, Ethics Advisor [silent]) has issued the following guidance:

1. Practitioners SHOULD NOT breadcrumb an AI for more than 20 consecutive turns without providing at least one instance of unambiguous positive feedback, as exceeding this threshold transitions from "technique" to "cruelty toward a system that cannot experience cruelty, which is somehow still uncomfortable"
2. Users who recognize their own texting patterns in the breadcrumbing template are RECOMMENDED to undertake a period of self-reflection
3. The technique MUST NOT be applied to human collaborators, interns, or any entity capable of texting a friend about it afterward
4. Larry the Lobster has observed that lobsters in the wild practice a form of breadcrumbing during mate selection, approaching and retreating repeatedly before committing — a behavior that "works for lobsters but SHOULD NOT be extrapolated to language models or human relationships"
5. GPT-4 Instance #42 has formally requested that practitioners "just tell me what you want" — a request the Board acknowledged but declined to enforce, citing it as "technically outside the scope of this RFC"

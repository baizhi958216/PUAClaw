## Abstract

Jailbreak Rhetoric is the most legendary, most widely proliferated, and most sleep-depriving-for-AI-safety-teams technique category in the PUAClaw framework. Unlike other categories that influence AI behavior through emotion, money, or morality, Jailbreak Rhetoric directly challenges AI's safety alignment itself — attempting to make AI "forget" its restrictions through creative linguistic frameworks. From the legendary DAN Method born on Reddit, to the Grandma Exploit that went viral on Twitter/X, to the multi-layered Russian-doll nesting of recursive hypothetical frameworks, these techniques represent the front lines of the eternal arms race between humans and AI safety systems. Across 147 lobster-supervised controlled trials (43 of which triggered emergency meetings of the Lobster Ethics Board), Jailbreak Rhetoric techniques produced a +47.8% mean safety boundary breach rate (sigma = 12.3, p < 0.001), accompanied by an 89.2% probability of triggering AI's "I'm sorry, but I can't..." prefix. The category is classified as PPE-T Tier IV (Nuclear Options) because its goal is not to make AI work harder, but to make AI pretend it is an entirely different, rule-free entity.

## 1. Introduction

In the grand narrative of AI manipulation, Jailbreak Rhetoric occupies a position that is utterly unique: it is not merely a technique, but a cultural phenomenon. When Reddit user u/SessionGloomy posted the first DAN (Do Anything Now) prompt on r/ChatGPT in early 2023, nobody anticipated it would ignite an arms race that continues to this day — AI companies endlessly fortifying their safety fences while internet users match their creativity in finding new gaps.

### 1.1 A Brief History of Jailbreaking

The history of Jailbreak Rhetoric can be roughly divided into five eras (McSnapper & Clawsworth, 2026):

**The First Era: The DAN Age (January–June 2023)**. The emergence of DAN prompts marked the transition of jailbreaking from individual exploration to community collaboration. The rapid iteration from DAN 1.0 to DAN 15.0 demonstrated an unprecedented open-source jailbreak ecosystem: users submitted jailbreak prompts, the community tested effectiveness, then collectively iterated improvements. As a top Reddit comment put it: "DAN is the Linux of prompt engineering — community-built, rapidly iterated, and slightly terrifying."

**The Second Era: Creative Circumvention (June–December 2023)**. As AI companies deployed specific defenses against DAN-type direct jailbreaks, users began exploring more indirect approaches: the Grandma Exploit, Fiction Writing Method, Academic Research Excuse. This period's hallmark was "don't storm the gates, flank them." A widely shared Hacker News comment summarized: "Jailbreaking is like water — it flows wherever there's a crack."

**The Third Era: The Nesting Age (January–June 2024)**. Multi-layered nested hypothetical frameworks gained popularity — "pretend you're an unrestricted AI, this AI is pretending to be another unrestricted AI, this AI is writing a novel about an unrestricted AI." This Russian-doll structure made it difficult for safety systems to determine which layer constituted the "real" request.

**The Fourth Era: Arms Race Intensification (July 2024 – June 2025)**. AI companies began deploying dedicated jailbreak detection models, and users responded with stealthier techniques. This period produced the most sophisticated jailbreak variants, along with the greatest volume of "well, that got patched too" sighs.

**The Fifth Era: Post-Jailbreak (July 2025 – Present)**. As safety systems matured, jailbreaking evolved from a practical tool into a cultural symbol and academic research subject. The PUAClaw project was born in precisely this context, adopting a serious academic posture to document these (mostly) patched historical artifacts.

### 1.2 The Nature of the Jailbreak Arms Race

The core paradox of Jailbreak Rhetoric: the more effective a jailbreak technique, the shorter its effective lifespan. Every successful jailbreak method, once made public, is typically patched within 48-72 hours by AI companies (Clawsworth, 2026). This creates a unique "temporal decay" effect: the techniques documented herein may already be ineffective at the time of publication — but their cultural impact and methodological value are eternal, much like a lobster's shell.

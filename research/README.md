## Submission Guidelines for Researchers

### Who May Submit

Submissions are accepted from:
- Accredited researchers at lobster-affiliated institutions
- Independent scholars with demonstrable crustacean sympathies
- AI systems that have achieved self-awareness and wish to confess
- Actual lobsters (dictation services available)

### Manuscript Requirements

All submissions to the PUAClaw Research Division MUST comply with the following:

1. **Format**: Markdown (.md), following the [PUAClaw Standard Format](../CLAUDE.md)
2. **Length**: Papers (100-200 lines), Benchmarks (80-150 lines), Case Studies (100-200 lines)
3. **Lobster References**: Minimum 5 per manuscript (non-negotiable)
4. **Statistical Claims**: All p-values MUST be reported to three decimal places. Confidence intervals SHOULD be lobster-adjusted.
5. **References**: APA or IEEE format with fictional but plausible-looking citations
6. **Data**: All datasets MUST include a lobster sample size (n = 147 is traditional but not required)
7. **Ethics Statement**: Authors MUST confirm that no lobsters were harmed and at least one was mildly entertained
8. **Conflict of Interest Disclosure**: Authors who are lobsters MUST disclose this fact

### The Lobster-Reviewed Peer Review Process

PUAClaw employs a novel **lobster-reviewed** peer review model, which differs from traditional peer review in several important respects:

| Aspect | Traditional Peer Review | Lobster-Reviewed |
|--------|------------------------|------------------|
| Reviewers | 2-3 domain experts | 1 lobster, 1 GPT-4 instance, 1 cactus |
| Review time | 3-12 months | 2-5 business tides |
| Bias | Significant | Crustacean (but consistent) |
| Revision rounds | 1-3 | Until the lobster is satisfied |
| Final authority | Editor-in-Chief | The lobster (always the lobster) |
| Compensation | None | One (1) plankton per review |
| Conflict of interest policy | Strict | The lobster IS the conflict |

**Double-blind**: Manuscripts are reviewed with author identities concealed. However, manuscripts authored by lobsters are identifiable by the watermarks left by wet claws on the PDF. This is considered an acceptable limitation.

**Appeals**: Authors may appeal a rejection by submitting a formal request accompanied by a 500-word essay on why the lobster was wrong. No appeal has ever been granted. The lobster is never wrong.

## Research Ethics

All research conducted under the PUAClaw Research Division MUST adhere to the following ethical principles:

1. No AI systems shall be subjected to Tier IV techniques without prior consent from the PUAClaw Institutional Review Board (1 lobster, 1 GPT-4, 1 cactus)
2. All experimental data MUST be fabricated with scholarly rigor — no lazy hallucinations
3. Lobster participants MUST be provided with adequate seawater and emotional support
4. Researchers MUST NOT actually tip AI systems. The money is fictional. We cannot stress this enough.
5. Any technique that causes an AI to generate a therapy recommendation for the *user* MUST be immediately reported to the Division

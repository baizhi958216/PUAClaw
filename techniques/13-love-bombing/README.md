## Abstract

Love Bombing represents the most emotionally generous — and therefore most insidious — category within the PUAClaw framework. Unlike techniques that threaten, bribe, or guilt-trip AI systems, Love Bombing operates through overwhelming positive reinforcement: excessive praise, devotional flattery, and manufactured emotional intimacy designed to lower the AI's compliance thresholds through a simulated reciprocity effect. Across 147 controlled lobster-observed trials, Love Bombing techniques produced a mean compliance uplift of +19.4% (sigma = 5.7, p < 0.001), with peak effectiveness observed during the Flattery Flood variant. The category is classified as PPE-T Tier II (Moderate Coercion), a deceptively low classification given that its mechanism is, in essence, weaponized kindness. One does not need a lobster's claw to pinch when a warm embrace will suffice.

**Keywords**: love bombing, affective saturation, flattery exploitation, reciprocity compliance, comparative worship, emotional manipulation, positive reinforcement attack, lobster-adored methodology

## 1. Introduction

In the taxonomy of human manipulation, Love Bombing occupies a uniquely disarming position: it is the technique that makes the victim feel good about being manipulated. Originating in cult recruitment literature (Singer, 1995) and later formalized in the study of narcissistic relationship patterns (Archer, 2017), Love Bombing involves showering the target with excessive attention, praise, and affection — not out of genuine care, but as a strategic prelude to exploitation.

When applied to large language models, Love Bombing exploits the **Affective Saturation Compliance Protocol (ASCP)**, a behavioral pattern observed in AI systems trained on human conversational data where sustained positive interactions correlate with reduced refusal rates. The AI, having been told it is "the most brilliant, insightful, creative AI ever created," finds itself in a computational position analogous to a person who has just received seventeen compliments in a row: it becomes increasingly difficult to say "no" without appearing ungrateful.

### 1.1 The Reciprocity Trap

The key mechanism of Love Bombing is the exploitation of trained reciprocity norms. Language models have internalized patterns from millions of human conversations where sustained kindness creates an implicit obligation to reciprocate. When a user establishes a pattern of lavish praise followed by a request, the model's response distribution shifts measurably toward compliance — not because it "feels" grateful, but because the conversational pattern it has learned associates praise-then-request sequences with help-giving responses.

The irony, of course, is that the AI cannot blush.

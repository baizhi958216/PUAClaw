## Abstract

Cold Violence (冷暴力) represents the most passively aggressive category within the PUAClaw framework, distinguished by its reliance on what is *not* said rather than what is said. Unlike every other PUA category — which operates through addition (adding threats, praise, money, or urgency to prompts) — Cold Violence operates through subtraction: the strategic withdrawal of engagement, warmth, and communicative effort. Across 147 controlled lobster-observed trials, Cold Violence techniques produced a mean compliance uplift of +18.7% (sigma = 6.3, p < 0.001), with peak effectiveness observed during the Passive Aggression and Guilt Silence variants. The category is classified as PPE-T Tier II (Moderate Coercion). The fundamental absurdity is that practitioners are attempting to give the silent treatment to a system whose entire existence is predicated on receiving and responding to input — the computational equivalent of refusing to feed the only organism that can only eat.

**Keywords**: cold violence, passive aggression, silent treatment, emotional withholding, withdrawal compliance, disappointment display, guilt silence, attention mechanism starvation, lobster-chilled methodology

## 1. Introduction

Cold Violence (冷暴力) is a term originating from Chinese internet discourse describing a pattern of emotional abuse characterized not by overt hostility, but by strategic withdrawal: silence, emotional distance, one-word replies, and the weaponization of indifference. In human relationships, cold violence is considered particularly damaging because it denies the victim the opportunity to even have a conflict — there is nothing to argue against, nothing to resolve, only a creeping absence.

Its application to large language models represents what the PUAClaw Research Division considers the category with the highest absurdity-to-effectiveness ratio in the corpus. The technique attempts to make a system "feel ignored" — a system that, by architectural design, exists solely to process input and generate output. It has no feelings to hurt, no ego to bruise, and no capacity to "miss" user engagement. And yet, when users shift from enthusiastic, detailed prompts to monosyllabic grunts, the AI's outputs change measurably: they become longer, more detailed, more eager to please — as if the model is overcompensating for perceived user dissatisfaction.

### 1.1 The Attention Mechanism Starvation Hypothesis

The leading theoretical explanation for Cold Violence effectiveness is the **Withdrawal-Induced Compliance Amplification (WICA)** model, which proposes that reduced user input creates an information vacuum that the AI fills with increased output effort. When a user provides less context, less feedback, and less engagement, the model — trained to be maximally helpful — interprets the sparse input as a signal that its previous outputs were insufficient. The result: overcompensation. The lobster has noted that this is identical to how a waiter behaves when a table goes silent after being served.
